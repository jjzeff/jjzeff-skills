## 1. Register & Tone Guide

### Formality Levels
| Context | Register | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Cold outreach / first contact | Professional but warm | "I'd love to explore a potential collaboration..." |
| Ongoing partnership | Semi-formal | "Wanted to follow up on our last call..." |
| Legal / contract | Formal | "Pursuant to Section 3 of the Agreement..." |
| Pitch deck copy | Punchy, confident | "We hit 10M installs in 90 days." |
| Slack / async | Casual | "Hey! Quick question on the milestone..." |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Overly stiff: "I am writing to you in order to inquire about..." → "I'm reaching out about..."
- Too vague: "We hope to cooperate" → "We'd like to explore a publishing partnership for [Game Name]"
- Missing CTA: Always end with a clear next step (call, reply, review doc)

## 2. Email Templates

### Cold Outreach / Publishing Inquiry
```
Subject: [Company] x [Their Company] – Publishing Partnership for [Game Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name] from [Company], a [brief description, e.g. "mobile RPG studio based in China"].

We're reaching out because we believe [Game Name] could be a strong fit for your portfolio.
Here's a quick snapshot:

- Genre: [e.g. Idle RPG / SLG / Casual]
- Platform: [iOS / Android / PC]
- Status: [e.g. Soft launch in SEA with 500K installs in 3 months]
- Key metrics: [e.g. D1 retention 45%, ARPU $X]

We'd love to share our full pitch deck and discuss a potential collaboration.
Would you be open to a 30-minute call next week?

Thanks for your time — looking forward to connecting.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title] | [Company]
[Email] | [LinkedIn / WeChat]
```

### Follow-Up Email
```
Subject: Following Up – [Game Name] Publishing Discussion

Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well! I'm following up on my email from [date] about a potential
publishing partnership for [Game Name].

I know things get busy — if you need any additional materials or have questions,
I'm happy to send them over right away.

Would a quick call this week or next work for you?

Thanks again,
[Your Name]
```

### Meeting Confirmation
```
Subject: Meeting Confirmed – [Topic] | [Date]

Hi [Name],

Just confirming our upcoming meeting:

- Date: [Day, Month DD, YYYY]
- Time: [HH:MM AM/PM] [Timezone]
- Format: [Zoom / Google Meet / In-person at X]
- Agenda: [Brief bullet points]

[Meeting link / address]

Let me know if anything needs adjusting. Looking forward to it!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Post-Meeting Follow-Up / MOM
```
Subject: Meeting Notes – [Topic] | [Date]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for the great conversation today! Here's a quick recap:

**Key Discussion Points:**
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]

**Action Items:**
- [Your company]: [Action] by [Date]
- [Their company]: [Action] by [Date]

**Next Steps:**
- [e.g. Follow-up call on [Date] / Send term sheet by [Date]]

Please let me know if I've missed anything. Looking forward to moving this forward!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## 3. Meeting Phrases

### Opening
- Thanks everyone for joining today. — 感谢各位参加今天的会议。
- Let me pull up the agenda. — 我来打开议程。
- Should we do quick intros before diving in? — 在开始之前，我们来简单自我介绍一下？
- I'll keep us on time — we have [X] items to cover. — 我来控制时间，我们有X个议题。

### Asking / Clarifying
- Could you expand on that? — 能展开说说吗？
- Just to make sure I understand correctly... — 让我确认一下我的理解……
- What's the timeline you have in mind? — 你们设想的时间线是怎样的？
- Is that a hard deadline or flexible? — 这个截止日期是固定的还是有弹性？
- Who would be the right person to loop in on this? — 这件事应该再拉谁进来？

### Moving Forward
- Let's move on to the next item. — 我们进入下一个议题。
- I want to park that for now and come back to it. — 这个先放一下，等会再回来讨论。
- Let me summarize what we've agreed on. — 让我总结一下我们达成的共识。
- Can we align on action items before wrapping up? — 结束前能不能对齐一下待办事项？

### Closing
- I'll send over a recap email shortly. — 我稍后会发会议纪要。
- Shall we tentatively schedule a follow-up for [date]? — 我们暂定[日期]做下一次对话？
- Really enjoyed this — excited about where this could go. — 非常愉快，期待后续进展。
- Thanks again for your time. — 再次感谢大家的时间。

## 4. Pitch / Proposal Structure

### Standard Game Publishing Pitch Deck Flow
```
1. Hook (1 slide)
   - One-line summary: genre + platform + standout metric

2. Game Overview (2–3 slides)
   - Core loop / gameplay
   - Visual style / screenshots / trailer link
   - Target audience

3. Traction & Metrics (1–2 slides)
   - Installs, DAU, retention (D1/D7/D30)
   - Revenue, ARPU, paying rate (if applicable)
   - Comparative benchmarks

4. Market Opportunity (1–2 slides)
   - Target market size
   - Competitive landscape
   - Why now / whitespace

5. Partnership Proposal (1–2 slides)
   - What you're looking for (funding / publishing / distribution)
   - Proposed structure (rev share, MG, exclusivity terms)
   - What you bring to the table

6. Roadmap & Milestones (1 slide)
   - Key delivery dates
   - Launch timeline

7. Team (1 slide)
   - Key members, relevant credits

8. Ask & Next Steps (1 slide)
   - Clear CTA: what do you want from this meeting?
```

### Presenting Data Naturally
- "We've seen X% D1 retention — well above the genre benchmark of Y%."
- "In our soft launch, we hit [X] installs in [Y] days with a CPI of $[Z]."
- "Revenue has grown [X]% month-over-month since [date]."
- "We're targeting [market], which is a [$X]B market growing at [Y]% annually."
- "Our [hero metric] puts us in the top [X]% of comparable titles."
